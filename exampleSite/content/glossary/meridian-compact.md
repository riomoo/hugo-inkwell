The Meridian Compact was a loose confederation of twelve seagoing city-states that shared a common trade language, a common law of the sea, and a common interest in whatever lay beyond the edge of charted water.

It was founded in the year 1201 by the harbor-wardens of seven cities, who signed the Compact on the deck of a ship in the middle of the open sea, equidistant from all of their home ports, because none of them trusted any of the others enough to sign it on land.

It lasted two hundred and eleven years.

## The Silence of 1412

The Compact dissolved during a period known as the Silence — fourteen months during which communication between member cities inexplicably ceased, ships stopped returning from certain routes, and {{< char name="Veth" slug="veth" >}}'s home city of Aehr sank beneath the sea.

The cause of the Silence is not recorded. The scholars who might have recorded it did not survive it.

## Legacy

Three centuries later, the Compact exists only in historical records and in the memories of {{< term term="Aehr" >}}'s dead, who remember it as a living thing and have not entirely adjusted to its absence.
