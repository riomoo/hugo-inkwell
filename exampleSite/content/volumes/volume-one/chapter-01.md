The water was the color of old bronze when Sael came ashore.

She had no memory of the crossing — only the cold, and the particular silence of deep water pressing against her ears, and then the sand. Salt-white sand, fine as ground bone, and above it a city that rose from the sea like something the tide had forgotten to take back.

{{< scene-break >}}

Aehr had drowned three hundred years ago. Every cartographer agreed on this. Every sailor who'd sailed those waters had nothing to report but flat, featureless sea. And yet here it was: towers of pale coral and black basalt, bridges spanning canals that still ran with slow dark water, lanterns burning behind windows of sea-glass in colors she had no names for.

Sael wrung brine from her coat and tried to remember how she had gotten here.

{{< callout type="lore" title="The Drowned City of Aehr" >}}
Aehr was the capital of the Meridian Compact, a confederation of seagoing city-states that collapsed during the Silence of 1412. Its drowning is recorded in the *Annals of the Outer Shore* as sudden and total — "gone between one tide and the next." No survivors were recorded.
{{< /callout >}}

The harbor was occupied. Not by ships — she saw none — but by people moving with the unhurried certainty of those who have lived somewhere long enough to stop noticing it. A woman selling fish from a flat-bottomed boat. Two children chasing a gull. A man mending a net with fingers that moved without thought, his eyes on the middle distance.

None of them looked at her.

None of them left wet footprints.

She looked down at her own boots: dark with water, leaving clear marks on the pale stone of the quay. She looked at the fish-seller's bare feet: dry, and faintly luminous, like skin seen through shallow water.

*Oh,* she thought. *Oh, I see.*

{{< scene-break >}}

"You're thinking we're dead," said a voice behind her.

Sael turned. A woman leaned against the harbor wall — or rather, leaned *through* it, one shoulder passing into the stone as though it were nothing more than shadow. She was dressed in the fashion of three centuries ago: high collar, wide sleeves, a coat the color of deep water. Her eyes were the same.

"The city's dead," the woman said. "We're something else." She smiled. It was not unkind. "You, on the other hand, are extremely alive. You've been dripping on my harbor for ten minutes. It's very distracting."

"I apologize," Sael said, because she had been raised to be polite even to impossible things.

"Don't. It's been a long time since anything dripped." The woman pushed off from the wall and walked toward her — through a merchant's cart, through the merchant, who shivered and pulled his coat closer without looking up. "My name is {{< char name="Veth" slug="veth" >}}. I used to be the harbor-warden of Aehr. Now I am the harbor." She tilted her head. "And you are the answer to a question I have been asking for a very long time."

Sael looked at the drowned city rising around her, at the dead moving through it like light through water, at the strange pale sky that seemed too close and too bright and too *still*.

"What was the question?" she asked.

{{< char name="Veth" slug="veth" >}} smiled again — wider this time, with something in it that was almost hunger, almost hope, almost the look of someone who has been waiting at a door for three hundred years and has just heard footsteps.

"*How does something drowned learn to breathe again?*"
