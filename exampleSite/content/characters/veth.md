Veth has been dead for three hundred years and takes it with remarkable equanimity.

She was the harbor-warden of {{< term term="Aehr" >}} when the city drowned — the last official to sound the alarm, the last to leave her post, the first to realize there was nowhere to leave *to*. She has spent the centuries since keeping the city in order, managing its dead, and waiting for something she could not name but always suspected was coming.

## Nature

As the dead of Aehr lose their weight and warmth gradually over years, Veth has lost more than most — she can pass through solid matter, and her eyes have taken on the deep-water quality of something very old that has spent a long time in the dark.

She is also, in some sense that the story does not explain cleanly until later, *the harbor itself*. The city's survival as a ghost is tied to her continued presence and will.

## Voice

Veth speaks the way people do when they have had three centuries to choose their words carefully. She is precise, dry, occasionally funny, and entirely without self-pity about being dead. She finds Sael's tendency to drip on things endearing.

## The Bottle

She carries a sealed glass bottle containing {{< spoiler >}}the moment of her own death — specifically, the memory of the last breath she took before the water came in. This is the key to the Meridian door.{{< /spoiler >}}
